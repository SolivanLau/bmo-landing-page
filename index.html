How it works

Get all the flexibility and convenience that comes with BMO PaySmart, whether you buy in-store or online.

Enjoy lower monthly payments with no interest and a low monthly fee. Convert your recent qualifying purchase into a BMO PaySmart Installment Plan through the BMO Mobile App or BMO Online Banking today.

  1. Log in to your account

    Sign in to Online Banking or Mobile Banking.

  2. Select a purchase

    Choose an eligible credit card purchase you'd like to convert into a PaySmart plan. Or you can go to the Installment Plan tab and click Create to view a list of all qualifying transactions.

  3. Choose a plan

    Pick between the 3, 6, or 12 month options and review the monthly fee (up to 0.9%†1) that applies - no interest.

  4. Give consent

    Read and agree to the BMO PaySmart Terms and Conditions.

BMO PaySmart Credit Card Installment Plan FAQs

How does a BMO PaySmart plan work?

A BMO PaySmart installment plan on your credit card lets you pay off a certain dollar amount in equal monthly payments over a set time frame of your choosing. This feature is available as part of your existing credit card and doesn't require a credit application.

The type of fees associated with your installment plan will depend on when you set it up:

  • At checkout (online or in store with participating merchants): You'll be enrolled in an interest-bearing plan with interest charges on your installment payments. You'll be provided with the total payment, monthly breakdown, and the interest rate (APR percentage). Each month, you'll pay the principal and interest as part of your minimum payment.

  • After purchase using BMO Online or Mobile Banking: You'll be enrolled in a fee-based plan with no interest. Instead, a monthly fee, shown during set up, is applied based on a percentage of your original purchase amount. Each month, you'll pay the principal and the fee as part of your credit card minimum payment.

How do I know if i qualify for a BMO PaySmart installment plan?

You qualify for a BMO PaySmart plan if:

  • You're the primary cardholder of an eligible BMO credit card
  • Your account is in good standing
  • You recently made an eligible purchase of $100 or more with your eligible BMO credit card†3
  • You're registered for BMO Online Banking and have an email address on file
  • You haven't reached the maximum number of installment plans allowed

During checkout, you'll be shown the list of installment plan options for qualifying purchases with participating merchants. To qualify after purchases have been made, transactions need to have been posted to your credit card account but not yet shown on your latest statement

Will I be charged interest on my installment plan?

This will depend on when you create your installment plan:

  • At checkout (online or in store with participating merchants):

    You'll be charged interest on your plan, with the (APR) percentage displayed at setup.

  • At checkout (online or in store with participating merchants):

    You won't be charged interest on installment plans. If your plan gets cancelled, you'll be charged your standard purchase interest rate on your remaining balance.

Can I setup several installment plans? is there a limit for how many I can have?

Yes, you can have several active installment plans at the same time. There is a limit of 99 plans that you can have active at any given time.

What if I want to cancel my plan?

You can cancel your plan at any time, without penalty, through BMO Online Banking by following these steps:

  1. Select your credit card and click on the installment plan tab.
  2. Under “active plans”, choose the plan you want to cancel.
  3. Click the cancel button.

If you cancel your plan, any remaining balance will be added to your credit card balance and be subject to your standard interest rate as of the cancellation date. We will not charge you any more monthly fees once the plan is cancelled. Please note that you won't be able to re-instate your installment plan for the same purchase.

Keep in mind that if you return or receive a credit for an item that you put on an installment plan, your plan may not automatically get cancelled. The credit amount is applied to the overall balance on your account and not directly to the specific amount of the installment plan. For more information on how we apply payments to your account, refer to the terms and conditions.

Terms & Conditions

BMO PaySmart installment plans allow primary cardholders to repay specific eligible amounts in equal monthly payments over a defined repayment schedule subject to the conditions set out in the terms and conditions. The applicable fees will be less than or equal to 0.9% of your principal amount and will be presented to you at the time of plan set-up. The monthly fee charged is less than or equal to the interest you might have paid if you carried this balance on your credit card account for the equivalent time of your plan duration. Installment plans are not a special rate offer. Purchases or balances converted to installment plans continue to be counted towards your available credit limit. See the full terms and conditions. All PaySmart rates, fees and terms are subject to change.

Setting up an installment plan won’t impact your credit score, as long as you pay your minimum payment on time. When you set up a BMO PaySmart plan, we will not perform a credit check.

“Recent” purchases refer to those that have been posted to your credit card account but have not yet appeared on your latest credit card statement.
